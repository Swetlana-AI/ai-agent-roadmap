An AI agent (or chatbot, or support bot — same thing) is software that talks to your customers on your behalf. It answers questions, qualifies leads, books calls, and routes anything it can't handle to a human. For a small business, a good one runs 24/7, speaks every language your customers do, and handles the repetitive conversations that eat your evenings.

The Four Mechanics of Every Agent
1
Channel
Where the conversation lives — your website, WhatsApp, Instagram, etc.
2
Brain
The LLM (GPT, Claude, Gemini) that understands and generates responses.
3
Knowledge
Your content — website, PDFs, FAQs — fed to the brain so it knows your business.
4
Actions
What it can do — collect emails, book meetings, hand off to a human.
The flow: A customer sends a message through the channel. The brain reads it, checks your knowledge for relevant facts, writes a reply, and triggers an action if needed (like saving a lead or sending a Calendly link). Every platform — Chatbase, ManyChat, Voiceflow — is just a different wrapper around these same four pieces.
What You Don't Need to Worry About

You don't need to train a model, write code, or understand vector databases to ship something useful. Modern no-code platforms handle embeddings, retrieval, and hosting for you — your job is to make good decisions about what the bot does, where it lives, and what it knows. That's exactly what this wizard walks you through.

Every agent hits edge cases. Your "escape hatch" is where AI hands off to a human — and where real conversions happen.
